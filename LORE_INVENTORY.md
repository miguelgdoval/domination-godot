## §1. The World — Foundational Claims

### The Perpetual Chronometer (the universe)

> "The universe is the **Perpetual Chronometer** (*El Cronómetro Perpétuo*) — a vast, impossibly complex clockwork machine that holds the very fabric of reality together. Time is not abstract here; it is a geared substance that flows in ordered cycles. The Chronometer maintains this order. The Machine is perfect. It is also dying."
> — GDD

> "A vast, impossibly complex clockwork machine that holds the very fabric of reality together. Time is not abstract within the Chronometer; it is a geared substance that flows in ordered cycles. The Machine is perfect. The Machine is also dying. The Society of Time Architects exists to slow the dying. The Operator exists to extract Mastery from the Machine's failures, so that future Operators may survive them. **Nobody — not the Architects, not the Archiver, not the Renegade — knows what the Chronometer was for, before it began to fail.**"
> — Codex: "The Perpetual Chronometer"

### Entropy (the conflict)

> "The Machine suffers from chronic **Entropy** — a structural decay that cannot be stopped, only managed. The Entropy manifests in the simulations as increasing resistance, impossible targets, and corrupted flow states."
> — GDD

> "Entropy is the natural state toward which the Chronometer drifts. The Trial Cycles do not reverse it; they slow it. The Operator's task is explicitly framed by the Architects as 'recalibration' — restoring the Machine to function, not health. The Entropy Failures encountered during a Trial Cycle are not abstract. They are paradoxes the Machine has begun to embody. When an Operator isolates a Failure, the paradox is bound; the Machine learns to expect it. The same Failure will return, milder, in future Cycles. **This is not victory. This is delay.**"
> — Codex: "Entropy"

### Chronos (the measurement)

> "Chronos is not a currency; it is a measurement. When a Cohesion Pulse holds, the Machine extracts the temporal energy that pulse stabilised, and that energy is recorded as Chronos. The Operator who reaches a round's Chronos target has demonstrated that their Pulse was sufficient. The Operator who falls short has not. Chronos cannot be spent. Only its stable byproducts — Coins — can."
> — Codex: "Chronos"

### Recalibration (victory)

> "The Trial Cycle is not played to win. It is played to recalibrate. A Recalibration is the Chronometer's acceptance that the Operator's completed Cycle has updated the Machine's models — that future Trial Cycles will be subtly different, subtly more stable, because of this one. **The Operator who Recalibrates does not save the universe. The Operator who Recalibrates delays its end by an amount the Architects cannot measure but believe to be non-zero.** That is the work."
> — Codex: "Recalibration"

### Title-screen tagline

> "The Chronometer falters. You are the Operator. Calibrate the signal before entropy claims the age."
> — `main.gd` title screen

## §2. The Cast — NPCs

### The Trial Operator (the player)

> "You are not a hero. You are **The Trial Operator** (*El Operario de Ensayo*) — a highly qualified engineer recruited into the Society of Time Architects, a secret organization responsible for maintaining the Chronometer. Your task is precise and critical: take a seat at the Observation Window — the play table — and run timeline simulations. Each simulation tests the Machine's resistance to structural failure. If the simulation holds, the Machine learns. If it collapses, the data is preserved and the simulation resets. **You are not unique. You are indispensable.**"
> — GDD

> "*'You are not a hero. You are a tool. The Society of Time Architects recruits engineers because heroes ask questions and engineers do not.'* The Operator's task is precise: take a seat at the Observation Window, configure the Calibration Core, accept a Protocol, and run a Trial Cycle. If the simulation holds, the Machine learns. If it collapses, the data is preserved and the simulation resets. You are not unique. You are indispensable."

## §3. Places — The Chambers

### The Observation Window

> "*'La Ventana de Observación del Tiempo.'* The play table. The Operator's only fixed location during a Trial Cycle. The Window does not display the simulation directly. It displays the simulation's projections — Temporal Flow Components arranged as the Machine processes them, scored as the Machine learns. The Operator's role is to insert flows into the Processing Core; the Window shows what the Machine does with them. **Operators report that the Window's brass fittings warm to the touch in successful Trial Cycles. The Architects have no recorded explanation.**"
> — Codex: "The Observation Window"

### Etapa I — The Mahogany Trial

> "The opening chamber. The Chronometer is most stable here; the simulation behaves close to its original calibration. Operators new to the work always begin in the Mahogany Trial. The targets are forgiving. The Frequency Drain manifests at the close of the Trial — a paradox of compressed Isolation Chambers, the Machine's first complaint about its own decay. **The Architects refer to the Mahogany Trial as 'the cradle.' The Archiver does not.**"
> — Codex: "The Mahogany Trial"

Aesthetic: Polished mahogany, brass fittings, warm candlelight. *El Ensayo de la Caoba.*

### Etapa II — The Industrial Load

> "The second chamber. The Chronometer's intermediate processes — pressure, amplification, the conversion of stable Cohesion Pulses into deeper calibration data. Here the Mirror Decay appears: pip values invert across the threshold. The Operator who has trusted high pips and resonant doubles up to this point must abandon those instincts for the Trial that follows. **The Decay is older than the Architects who study it.**"
> — Codex: "The Industrial Load"

Aesthetic: Steel and steam, copper pipes, industrial lamps. *La Carga Industrial.*

### Etapa III — The Cold Singularity

> "The deep chamber. Operators who reach the Singularity report the same thing: **the temperature drops, and the Machine breathes slower here.** The Resonance Inversion lives in this chamber. The Operator's most reliable scoring engine — stacked doubles — turns on them. The Trial becomes a test not of skill but of adaptation. The Operators who cannot abandon a winning strategy do not leave the Singularity. **The Architects have no records of this chamber's original purpose. The Renegade Mechanic claims to have one, but will not share it.**"
> — Codex: "The Cold Singularity"

Aesthetic: Black obsidian, neon green accents, cold white light. *La Singularidad Fría.*

### Etapa IV — The Archiver's Core (Hard mode only)

> "The deepest chamber. Reached only by Operators who have hardened their Trial Cycle — accepted the additional Etapa, the additional Failure. **This is where the Archiver lives.** The Operator who arrives here has earned the Archiver's full attention, and that attention is not pleasant. The Ghost Chain manifests in this chamber: the Archive forgets what it has seen, and you must remember the rest. **The Recalibration achieved in this chamber is not the same Recalibration achieved in the Singularity. Operators who have done both will not discuss the difference.**"
> — Codex: "The Archiver's Core"

Aesthetic: Ancient paper, brass, encrypted glyphs. *El Núcleo del Archivero.*

### The Brass Emporium

> "Not a physical store. A temporal intersection point — a window into a different part of the Machine where the Operator can exchange resources."
> — GDD

Run by **The Voice of the Emporium**. 3 item slots, random rarities, refreshes between rounds.

### The Artisan's Workshop

The Forge. Appears after every Entropy Failure (boss). Run by **The Master of the Forge**, with occasional swap visits from **The Renegade Mechanic**. 2 item slots: one guaranteed Ivory, one guaranteed Obsidian.

## §4. Core Mechanics with Lore Frame

### Temporal Flow Components (tiles)

> "Each Component encodes a fragment of a timeline. The pip values on its two faces represent Chronos charges — the raw stability of that flow. **Components are not symbols. They are pieces of the Machine, briefly removed from its core, placed in the Operator's Isolation Chamber for insertion into the Processing Core.** Each Component returns to the Machine at the end of the Trial Cycle, none the worse for the journey. The blank (0) face is not absent. It is dormant — a Universal Connector that accepts any input signal. **The Architects who study these tiles consider blanks the most important Components in the set.**"
> — Codex: "Temporal Flow Component"

### Cohesion Pulse (chain)

> "The chain of Temporal Flow Components the Operator constructs at the Observation Window. A Pulse is stable when its connecting pip values align — when the Machine recognises continuity across the inserted flows. **The Architects believe — without proof — that Singularity-tier Pulses are what the Chronometer was built to process.**"
> — Codex: "Cohesion Pulse"

### Branching at Doubles (anomaly)

> "A double tile placed in a Cohesion Pulse — two faces of identical pip value — does not behave like other Components. Its perpendicular orientation in the Pulse exposes additional faces, additional places the Operator can extend the chain. The Machine treats each placed double as a branching node. The chain is no longer a simple line; it is a small tree. **The Architects believe this is not a feature of dominoes. They believe this is a feature of timelines.**"
> — Codex: "Branching at Doubles"

### Double Resonance

> "A double Component — two matching pip faces on the same Component — resonates with itself when routed through the Core. The Machine interprets this self-reference as a coherence pulse of higher order, and grants the Operator a flat amplification on the next signal. **Doubles do NOT open additional matching ends; the Pulse remains a single line per the Architects' first-edition Component Manual. The Renegade Mechanic disagrees on this point, and his sketches of a 'branched Pulse' have been impounded by the Copper Guild on at least three occasions.**"
> — Codex: "Double Resonance"
> *(Note: this Codex entry CONTRADICTS the "Branching at Doubles" entry above. The contradiction is in-fiction: the Architects' manual disagrees with the Renegade. Both entries exist in the Codex simultaneously.)*

### The Wild

> "Wild tiles appear in only one Calibration Core — the Void Lattice — and rarely. They have no pip values. They connect to anything. **The Architects' position is that Wilds are calibration noise: imperfections in the Core's loading process, accepted by the Machine as inputs because the Machine accepts everything. The Renegade Mechanic disagrees. He says the Wilds are intentional. He has not said by whom.**"
> — Codex: "The Wild"

### Coins (Copper Guild currency)

> "When a Trial Cycle round completes successfully, the residual Chronos — the stable byproduct that cannot be reabsorbed by the Machine — is captured by the Copper Guild's contracts and minted into Coins. **The Guild's contracts predate the Society of Time Architects. The Architects do not understand them. The Architects pay anyway.** Coins can be spent at the Brass Emporium for Calibration Modules, or at the Artisan's Workshop for tile modifications. Unspent Coins at the end of a Cycle are returned to the Guild's vaults. They do not carry across Cycles."
> — Codex: "Coins"

### Calibration Core

> "Before each Trial Cycle, the Operator inserts a Calibration Core. The Core determines which Temporal Flow Components are available in the session — which timelines the Machine will be tested against. The Standard Core is a complete double-9 set. It is the calibration the Society recommends for all new Operators. The other Cores — Resonant, Dense, Void Lattice, and beyond — are restricted: the Society makes them available only as the Operator demonstrates sufficient mastery. **Eight Cores exist. Some Operators believe there are more. The Architects do not confirm this.**"
> — Codex: "Calibration Core"

### Protocol

> "The Protocolo de Ensayo modifies the fundamental rules of the Trial Cycle: hand size, number of plays, number of discards, opening Coins. Equilibrium is the baseline. Compression, Overload, and Cascade are alternative parameter sets, each rebalanced toward a different style of Operator. **The Society treats Protocols as cosmetic preferences. The Archiver does not.** *'A Protocol is a story you tell the Machine about how you will fail. The Machine listens.'*"
> — Codex: "Protocol"

### Simulation Failure (defeat)

> "'SIMULATION FAILURE. REINITIALIZING PROTOCOL. OPERATOR REMAINS AVAILABLE.' The Society does not call it death. The Society does not call it failure in the sense the Operator understands. The Trial Cycle collapses; the Operator is reset; the simulation begins again with whatever Mastery the Operator has accumulated. **The Operator's memory across Trial Cycles is, technically, intact. The Operator's certainty about that memory degrades with each Failure.** The Archiver tracks the count. The Operator does not need to ask why."
> — Codex: "Simulation Failure"

## §5. The Entropy Failures — Bosses

### Boss 1: Frequency Drain *(Mahogany)*

Mechanically: Hand size -1.

> "The first Failure the new Operator meets. Frequency Drain manifests as a compression of the Isolation Chamber: one tile fewer to work with, every hand. The Architects consider this the gentle Failure — the Machine is asking the Operator to demonstrate they can manage a reduction. Most Operators isolate it on their first attempt. Some do not. **Those who do not are not gone. They simply have not arrived yet.**"
> — Codex: "Frequency Drain"

Intro lore: "The frequencies bleed. What was stable becomes noise."

### Boss 2: Mirror Decay *(Industrial)*

Mechanically: Each pip scores as (9 − pip). High tiles weaken.

> "The Mirror does not damage the Pulse. The Mirror revises it. Each pip's chip contribution is inverted: a 9 scores as 0, a 0 scores as 9. The Operator who has built a chain of high-pip Components is punished. The Operator who has built blank-heavy or low-pip chains is rewarded. **The Architects believe Mirror Decay is older than the Machine.** The Archiver, asked about this, said only: '*The Machine learned the Mirror. The Mirror has always been.*'"
> — Codex: "Mirror Decay"

Intro lore: "Your signal reaches us fractured. The decay accelerates."

### Boss 3: Resonance Inversion *(Cold Singularity)*

Mechanically: Each double SUBTRACTS from mult instead of adding.

> "In the Singularity, self-referential flows have inverted polarity. The doubles that build the Operator's most reliable Pulses suddenly subtract from the multiplier. Five doubles in a chain become a wound. The Operator who isolates this Failure learns something about adaptation. The Operator who refuses to adapt — who insists on the build that has carried them — does not isolate it. **The Renegade Mechanic respects this Failure more than the others. He has never said why.**"
> — Codex: "Resonance Inversion"

Intro lore: "The mirror eats its reflection. What resonates now devours."

### Boss 4: Ghost Chain *(Archiver's Core — Hard only)*

Mechanically: ~1/3 of placed tiles fade from view. Pip values still apply.

> "The Archive forgets what it has seen. A third of the Operator's placed Components fade from the Observation Window — still scored, still connected, still in the Pulse, but no longer legible. The Operator must remember what is hidden. The Operator must connect future Components against pip values they cannot read. This is the final Failure. The Operator who isolates it has reached the Archiver's Core itself. **The Archiver does not congratulate them. The Archiver only opens the Core, and watches.**"
> — Codex: "Ghost Chain"

Intro lore: "The Archive remembers what it pleases. You must remember the rest."

## §6. The Five Transmissions — Major Lore Revelations

These are the deepest disclosures in the Codex. Each unlocks by deep milestone.

### Transmission #1 — *(unlocked at 5 wins)*

> *"Operator — this is the Archiver. You have been told that the Chronometer is dying. This is correct. **You have not been told that the Chronometer is dying because something is killing it. This is also correct.** The Society of Time Architects has chosen not to address the second fact. I have chosen to do so. There will be further transmissions."*

### Transmission #2 — *(unlocked at 7-day daily streak)*

> *"My designation was Operator-7. I held the Window for fourteen Cycles. I was the seventh Operator to complete the Archiver's Core. In the fifteenth Cycle the Mirror Decay reached for me before I reached for it. I had time to record this message before the Reinitialization began. **I do not know if I will be one of the Operators who remembers what happened.** If you are reading this — if you are not me — please stop trusting the high pips. The high pips are not your friends. They never were."*

### Transmission #3 — *(unlocked at 50 lifetime failures)*

> *"They will tell you the Chronometer was built to keep time. **This is the first lie.** The Chronometer was built to keep one specific moment. The Machine preserves an instant — a single span of seconds, perhaps less — across all possible timelines. Everything else, every other moment in every other timeline, is permitted to drift. The Machine does not care about those. The Trial Cycle is the Machine's way of asking: is the preserved moment still there? Each Recalibration confirms it is. Each Failure raises the question. **I would like very much to know what moment they are preserving.**"*

### Transmission #4 — *(unlocked at first Hard win)*

> *"You have done well, Operator. You have isolated every Failure the Machine has shown you. You have built the chains the Society did not believe could be built. Do you remember when you began? Do you remember the first Trial Cycle? Can you call the face of the first Architect who instructed you? **I cannot.** Operator — the Society has been running the Trial Cycles for a long time. I cannot remember how long. I cannot remember when I last remembered. **We are not the first ones to try this. We are not even the hundredth.**"*

### Transmission #5 — The Preserved Moment *(unlocked at 50 wins)*

> *"Operator. The fifth transmission. The Archive has counted your Recalibrations and there are fifty. This is enough. You asked once — through me, through the records left by Operator-prime, through the worn surface of the Window itself — what the Chronometer was preserving. The Archive may not answer that question. **The Archive may, on certain occasions, show:**
>
> ```
> [a brass lever, half-pulled]
> [a candle, three seconds from extinguishing]
> [an Operator's hand, leaving the Window]
>
> timestamp:  ████████████████
> ```
>
> These are Components of the moment. Not all of them. Not most. We have not yet fully reconstructed what is held here. The reconstruction is what your Trial Cycles are for. Continue, Operator. We are close. Or we are no closer than we have ever been. **I cannot tell from inside the Archive.**"*

## §11. The Event Terminal — 5 Encounters

### Event 1: The Archiver Observes a Flow *(round_index ≥ 2)*

**Speaker:** THE ARCHIVER

> *"Operator. I have observed your current array. One of your Components carries an anomaly that interests me. I will compensate you for surrendering it."*

- **B. Refuse the transaction** — outcome: *"The Archive notes the refusal. Nothing more."*
- **C. Offer information instead** — outcome: *"Your Cycle data is logged. The Guild deposits a small stipend."* (rep: +1 guild)

### Event 2: The Renegade's Workbench *(round_index ≥ 4)*

**Speaker:** THE RENEGADE MECHANIC

> *"Heard about your last Pulse. Here. Take this. Don't tell anyone where it came from. The Master would have to file paperwork."*

- **C. Trade your oldest Module for an Ivory one** — outcome: *"A clean swap. He keeps the old gear. It belongs to him now."* (rep: +2 renegade)

### Event 3: A Copper Guild Audit *(round_index ≥ 3)*

## §23. Internal Contradictions Already Present in Canon

The world has already planted self-contradictions. The bible should decide which way each resolves (or whether the contradiction is the point).

1. **Branching vs. linear chains.** The "Branching at Doubles" Codex entry says doubles open additional matching ends ("The Architects believe this is a feature of timelines"). The "Double Resonance" entry says they do NOT, per "the Architects' first-edition Component Manual." The contradiction is between two Architects' positions decades apart — possibly the Renegade's influence shifted the canon.

2. **Chronos Amplifier counts.** The Society holds three. The Renegade made the rest. Their counts disagree. Which is the real number?

3. **Resonance Chamber origin.** Renegade claims to have invented the principle. Society's records show otherwise "but only by a few decades." Who actually invented it?

4. **The Operator's identity.** Anonymous engineer recruited by the Society — BUT the Daily Memorial gives each Operator a unique number AND epitaph. Are players different Operators each run? The same Operator with degrading memory? Echoes of Operator-prime?

5. **The Mirror Decay's age.** Codex: "older than the Architects who study it." Archiver: "The Machine learned the Mirror. The Mirror has always been." Mirror is older than the Machine itself?

## §24. Tonal Inventory — How the World Sounds

The voice is consistent across all canon:

- **Sentences are short.** "The Machine learns nothing yet." "I cannot." "This is enough."
- **Negative phrasing.** Lots of "does not," "has not," "will not." The world is full of unfilled gaps.
- **Bureaucratic-archaic register.** "Filed a Notice of Withdrawal." "The audit closes early." "Records remain open." Crossed with technological-mystical vocabulary: "Cohesion Pulse," "Calibration Module," "Routing flows."
- **The Archive narrator is omniscient but not warm.** Every Codex entry reads like a clerk who has seen everything and decided to mention only a fraction.
- **Recurring rhetorical move: undermining the prior sentence.** "He has trained every Operator who has reached him. **He has not spoken at length to any.**" "The Society holds three of these. **The Renegade Mechanic admits to having made the rest.**" "The Architects insist this is psychosomatic. **The Renegade Mechanic agrees with them, then asks the Operator what note the tone hit.**"
- **Italics for direct character speech.** The cast is heard through quoted phrases, never through scene-style dialogue.
- **The world withholds.** Almost every fact comes with a sentence acknowledging what is NOT known. "The provenance is unrecorded." "The Architects, asked to comment, declined." "The reason has not been given."

## §25. What the World Does NOT Yet Have

Gaps the bible may want to fill, or deliberately preserve:

- **Geography.** No map. The chambers exist; how are they connected? Walking the corridors (Master) implies physical space.
- **Timekeeping.** "Cycles" are units of game-runs, not lived time. How long is a Cycle in-fiction? Hours? Years?
- **Population.** How many Operators exist concurrently? The Daily Memorial implies many; the Workshop is built for one.
- **Recruitment.** The Operator is "recruited" by the Society. How? From where?
- **The Architects themselves.** Plural noun, never depicted individually. Are they human? Are they alive?
- **The Chronometer's location.** Is it in the universe, or IS it the universe? The Codex says it "holds the very fabric of reality together." Ambiguous.
- **Death.** Operators "do not arrive yet." Failures "preserve data." There is no in-fiction description of permanent death — only of recurrence.
- **The outside.** Nothing exists in the lore outside the Chronometer-system. Is there an outside?
- **Motive.** Why does the Society maintain the Machine? Habit? Duty? Survival? Are they ALSO inside the preserved moment?
